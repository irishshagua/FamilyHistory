Notes for Catherine Linsenmeyer:

Born in Comar.  Joseph, her son, married a woman on the boat.  The first wife died and he sent for Catherine Linsenmeyer his mother to care for the child.  Joseph then married Mardalene Freywald.  According to Edwin Healy interview June 2005.


According to Edwin Healy interview June 2005, Catherine Linsenmeyer when the great Chicago fire began on Oct. 29, 1871 had gone up to the northside of Chicago to visit and friend and brought her "caps and gowns" from Colmar, France.  The people when she visited were told by the fire brigade to leave.  They loaded what they could and were not able to take the "caps and gowns".  At age 77 she was lost in the shuffle of the disarray of the fire for 3 days.  Catherine's son, Joseph found her.  She sat afterwards sitting a rocking chair and was known to repeat often "oh my beautiful caps and gowns."
